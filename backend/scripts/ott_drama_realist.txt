INT. GRAM PANCHAYAT OFFICE - PHULERA - DAY

A government office that is also a storage room
for chairs. Fans that move the heat around without
reducing it. A calendar from two years ago.

ABHISHEK TRIPATHI, 28, IAS-aspirant-turned-panchayat-
secretary, sits at a desk that arrived before he did
and will outlast him. He's reading a file.

PRAHLAD, 60s, peon, sweeps around him.

                    ABHISHEK
          Prahlad bhai. The toilet budget from
          last quarter — it shows thirty thousand
          spent but there are only two new toilets.
          The math is—

                    PRAHLAD
          The math is fine, sahib.

                    ABHISHEK
          The math is not fine. Two toilets
          cost thirty thousand? Each toilet—

                    PRAHLAD
          Transportation. Labor. Materials.
          The contractor. The contractor's cousin.
          The cousin's chai. The chai shop owner's
          birthday bonus—

                    ABHISHEK
          I understand what you're saying.

                    PRAHLAD
          The math is fine, sahib.

Abhishek closes the file. Opens it again.

                    ABHISHEK
          I'm going to ask for an audit.

Prahlad stops sweeping. Looks at him.

                    PRAHLAD
          Sahib. You've been here three months.

                    ABHISHEK
          I know.

                    PRAHLAD
          The contractor has been here thirty years.

                    ABHISHEK
          I'm aware.

                    PRAHLAD
          His son is the District MLA's brother-in-law.

                    ABHISHEK
          Also aware.

                    PRAHLAD
          And you're going to ask for an audit.

                    ABHISHEK
          Yes.

Prahlad resumes sweeping. He sweeps in a different
direction now. Away from Abhishek.

                    PRAHLAD
                    (quietly)
          The previous sahib lasted six months.

EXT. PHULERA VILLAGE - ROAD - DAY

A road that is also a suggestion. Potholes of
philosophical depth. Abhishek rides a bicycle
because the office jeep has a broken axle
since October and the repair order is "pending."

He passes the GRAM PRADHAN, MANJU DEVI, 50s,
who sits at her doorstep peeling vegetables.

                    ABHISHEK
          Pradhan ji. I need to discuss the
          toilet construction accounts.

                    MANJU DEVI
          Sit down and have some dal.

                    ABHISHEK
          I've already had lunch.

                    MANJU DEVI
          Then sit down and watch me have some dal.
          I don't like to eat alone.

He sits. He knows this will take a while.

                    MANJU DEVI
          The toilet accounts are fine.

                    ABHISHEK
          The toilet accounts are not fine.

                    MANJU DEVI
          Two new toilets for thirty thousand
          is not un-fine.

                    ABHISHEK
          Each toilet costs fifteen thousand?

                    MANJU DEVI
          Everything costs more.

                    ABHISHEK
          Than what?

                    MANJU DEVI
          Than what it looks like from Delhi.

She eats her dal. He watches her.

                    ABHISHEK
          Pradhan ji. This is government money.
          For government toilets. That people need.

                    MANJU DEVI
          I know what it is. I also know that
          this village has been waiting thirty years
          for a road. You know how the road gets built?

                    ABHISHEK
          Through proper channels, ideally—

                    MANJU DEVI
          Through the contractor. That contractor.
          You audit him, he stops working. You stop
          the contractor, the road doesn't get built.
          Not for another thirty years.

She offers him dal. He takes some.

                    MANJU DEVI (CONT'D)
          Your thirty thousand. How much was
          actually the toilets?

                    ABHISHEK
          Based on my estimates? Eight thousand.

                    MANJU DEVI
          And the other twenty-two?

                    ABHISHEK
          That's what I want to know.

                    MANJU DEVI
          The other twenty-two is why, next month,
          there will be a road proposal on your desk.
          A real one. That will actually move.
                    (pause)
          Everything costs more than it looks
          from Delhi, sahib. Everything costs
          exactly what it costs from here.

INT. DISTRICT COLLECTOR'S OFFICE - DAY

Abhishek sits across from the DISTRICT COLLECTOR,
52, who has not looked up from a file since
Abhishek entered.

                    COLLECTOR
          The toilet audit. You filed for one.

                    ABHISHEK
          Yes sir.

                    COLLECTOR
          I've received three calls about it.
          This morning.

                    ABHISHEK
          I see.

                    COLLECTOR
          The MLA's office. The Contractor's Association.
          And my wife, who plays kitty with the contractor's
          wife, and wanted to know what I was going to
          do about it.

He looks up.

                    COLLECTOR
          What are you going to do, Mr. Tripathi?

                    ABHISHEK
          Whatever you direct, sir.

                    COLLECTOR
          I'm directing you. To tell me what
          you want to do.

                    ABHISHEK
          I want to complete the audit.

                    COLLECTOR
          Why?

                    ABHISHEK
          Because the math is wrong.

The collector looks at him for a long moment.

                    COLLECTOR
          The math is always wrong, Mr. Tripathi.
          That's not why things move forward or stop.

He goes back to his file.

                    COLLECTOR (CONT'D)
          Audit approved. You have thirty days.
          I suggest you use them very carefully.

Abhishek rises.

                    ABHISHEK
          Thank you, sir.

                    COLLECTOR
          Don't thank me yet.

EXT. PHULERA VILLAGE - EVENING

Abhishek cycles home. The village at dusk:
smoke from cookfires, children playing, a dog
that has been following him since February.

He stops at the edge of a field. Looks at the road.
Imagines the road that could be here.

His phone rings. It's his MOTHER in Kanpur.

                    MOTHER (ON PHONE)
          When are you coming back?

                    ABHISHEK
          When the work is done.

                    MOTHER
          What work? You audit some files
          and call that work?

                    ABHISHEK
          It's more complicated than that.

                    MOTHER
          Your cousin Ritesh is in Bangalore.
          He earns four lakhs a month.

                    ABHISHEK
          I'm sure he does.

                    MOTHER
          You could have gone to Bangalore.

                    ABHISHEK
          I know.

                    MOTHER
          Why didn't you?

He looks at the field. He could explain.
He's not sure he can explain quickly enough
for a phone call.

                    ABHISHEK
          I'll call you Sunday, Ma.

He hangs up. The dog looks at him.

                    ABHISHEK (CONT'D)
          Don't ask.

He cycles on.

INT. DISTRICT COLLECTOR'S OFFICE - DAY

SANDEEP, 30s, IAS officer, third posting in four years, looks at the files stacked on his desk. They reach his chin. He has been here three hours and has not opened one.

His peon RAMPRASAD, 50s, brings tea.

                    RAMPRASAD
          The contractors are waiting, sir.
          Since nine o'clock.

                    SANDEEP
          What do they want?

                    RAMPRASAD
          The road tender, sir. They want to
          know who is getting it.

                    SANDEEP
          Tell them the committee decides.

                    RAMPRASAD
          The committee is them, sir.

Sandeep looks at the tea. Looks at the files. Looks at the door.

EXT. VILLAGE ROAD - DAY

Sandeep drives the government jeep himself. No peon, no assistant. The road turns to dirt after the highway. Then to mud.

He parks where the mud wins. Walks the rest.

An old woman is carrying bricks on her head to repair her own wall.

                    SANDEEP
          Where's the government contractor?

The woman sets down her bricks. Looks at him.

                    WOMAN
          Are you the government?

INT. LOCAL MLA'S OFFICE - NIGHT

SURESH MLA, 50s, garlands everywhere, photographs with important people. He is on the phone when Sandeep enters. Holds up one finger. Keeps talking.

Sandeep sits. Waits. Looks at the photographs.

Three minutes. Five. Eight.

Sandeep stands up and walks out.

Suresh MLA keeps talking.

EXT. VILLAGE WELL - MORNING

Women queue for water. The municipal tap has been dry for eleven days. Sandeep photographs it with his phone.

                    WOMAN IN QUEUE
          You'll take the photo and go.
          They all take photos and go.

                    SANDEEP
          I'm the collector.

                    WOMAN
          Last collector took our photo also.
          Tap is still broken.

INT. COLLECTOR'S OFFICE - NIGHT

Sandeep alone. Files open. He has found it — a diversion of funds. Clear, documented, undeniable.

He looks at the file for a long time.

He knows what happens to officers who file this report.

He files it anyway.

EXT. BUS STAND - DAWN

Six months later. Transfer orders in hand. Sandeep boards the state bus to his fourth posting. Different district, same problems.

A young man at the bus stop — college student, reading a newspaper — looks up at him.

                    YOUNG MAN
          Sir — aren't you the collector
          who filed the Ramdas case?

Sandeep looks at him.

                    SANDEEP
          I was.

The bus comes.
