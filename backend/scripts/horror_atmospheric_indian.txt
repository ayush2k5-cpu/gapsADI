HORROR ATMOSPHERIC INDIAN CORPUS
Source: Original screenplay excerpts in the style of 2010s Indian atmospheric folk horror
Genre: Horror | Era: Contemporary/OTT | Region: Indian

EXT. HAASPUR VILLAGE - DAWN - 1947

A village in the pre-partition dark. Mist
over the fields. But one house has lights
burning all night.

                    VILLAGER (V.O.)
          We do not speak of what Haaspur kept.
          We speak only of what it gave.
          And what it cost.

INT. HAASPUR HAVELI - BASEMENT - NIGHT

A room below a room. Stone walls. Old oil
lamps. A WOMAN, covered from head to toe, kneels
before something carved into the wall.

She speaks in a language older than the village.

The thing in the carving moves.

EXT. HAASPUR VILLAGE - PRESENT DAY

A dusty road. VINAYAK RAO, 30s, drives a rusted
jeep. He stops at a crossroads. The sign says:
HAASPUR — 2 KM.

His WIFE, PUJA, sits beside him with a map.

                    PUJA
          According to this there's nothing here.

                    VINAYAK
          My grandmother's house is here.

                    PUJA
          According to the map, your grandmother's
          house is in a field.

                    VINAYAK
          Then the map is wrong.

He drives on. The road gets narrower.
The trees close in.

INT. VILLAGE TEA SHOP - DAY

Two old men stop talking when Vinayak enters.

                    VINAYAK
          Namaskar. I'm looking for the Haaspur
          haveli. The Rao family house.

The two old men look at each other.

                    OLD MAN 1
          Rao family.

                    OLD MAN 2
                    (quietly)
          There is no one left from that family.

                    VINAYAK
          I'm from that family.

Silence. The tea in their glasses has stopped
steaming. They're looking at him like he said
something wrong. Like he said something
that couldn't be unsaid.

                    OLD MAN 1
          Which Rao?

                    VINAYAK
          My grandmother was Savitri Rao.

Both old men put down their glasses.

                    OLD MAN 1
          Go back to the city.

                    VINAYAK
          I inherited the property. I just want—

                    OLD MAN 1
          Go back to the city, bhai.
          Some inheritance you don't want.

EXT. HAASPUR HAVELI - AFTERNOON

The house is massive. Crumbling. Beautiful
in the way that very old dangerous things are beautiful.

Vinayak walks around it. Puja stays near the jeep.

The windows are all dark. There are no birds
on the roof. There are birds everywhere else.
Just not on this roof.

                    PUJA
          Vinayak. I don't like this.

                    VINAYAK
          It's just an old house.

                    PUJA
          Something about it feels—

                    VINAYAK
          I know. But it's ours. And I want to understand
          why my grandmother never talked about it.
          Why she left. What she left.

He tries the front door. It opens.

He goes in. Puja waits one minute. Then follows.
Because that's what you do.

INT. HAASPUR HAVELI - MAIN HALL - DAY

Dust. White sheets over furniture. Portraits
on the walls — ancestors going back generations.
All of them watching.

Vinayak stands before the last portrait.
A woman. Beautiful, severe. She's holding
something behind her back.

                    VINAYAK
          My grandmother.

He reaches up and lifts the portrait.
Behind it: a door.

                    PUJA
          We should leave.

                    VINAYAK
          Puja—

                    PUJA
          Vinayak. That door was behind a portrait.
          In a sealed room. In a house nobody has
          entered in fifty years.
                    (pause)
          We should leave.

He touches the door. His hand doesn't shake.
He wishes it would.

                    VINAYAK
          I need to know.

                    PUJA
          Some things are secrets for a reason.

The door opens inward.

Stairs. Going down.

INT. HAVELI BASEMENT - CONTINUOUS

The same room from 1947. The same carvings.
The same lamps — impossibly, still burning.

Vinayak stops at the bottom of the stairs.
The flames of the lamps bend toward him.

Against the wall: a box. Sealed and heavy.
And above it, carved deep into the stone:
a name. His grandmother's name.
And below it:

PAID. DO NOT TAKE WHAT WAS GIVEN.

                    VINAYAK
                    (whispering)
          Dadi... what did you give?

He reaches for the box.

INT. VILLAGE DOCTOR'S HOUSE - NIGHT

DOCTOR ANANTRAO, 70s, lights a lamp slowly.

Vinayak sits across from him. Puja stands
at the door. She hasn't sat down since the basement.

                    ANANTRAO
          Your grandmother made a bargain.
          Long ago. Before the partition.
          When the village was dying of drought.

                    VINAYAK
          What kind of bargain?

                    ANANTRAO
          The kind that keeps.

                    VINAYAK
          With what?

The doctor adjusts the lamp. The light goes
one way. His shadow goes another.

                    ANANTRAO
          With what lives in that house.
          What has always lived in that house.
          Your family kept it fed. Kept it quiet.
          When your grandmother left — when she
          ran — she thought distance would help.

                    VINAYAK
          Did it?

                    ANANTRAO
          It kept it quiet. For one generation.

He looks at Vinayak.

                    ANANTRAO (CONT'D)
          You're the second generation.

EXT. HAASPUR HAVELI - NIGHT

Puja stands outside the haveli. The lights
are on inside now. All the lights.

She didn't turn them on.

Vinayak is inside. He went back.
He said he'd be five minutes.

That was an hour ago.

She stands at the door. She will not go in again.
She knows she will.

The lights in the haveli pulse once.
Like a breath. Like a heartbeat.

She goes in.

EXT. VILLAGE EDGE - DUSK

The last house before the forest. A child draws a line in the mud and will not cross it.

                    CHILD
          Dadi says nobody goes past the line
          after dark.

ARJUN, 30s, city clothes, crosses the line.

                    CHILD
          She also says they always cross.

INT. ABANDONED HAVELI - NIGHT

Arjun's torch sweeps the room. Dust. Broken furniture. A cradle, still rocking.

No wind.

EXT. HAVELI GARDEN - NIGHT

Arjun photographs the outer walls. Through the viewfinder he sees a woman standing in the upstairs window.

He lowers the camera.

The window is bricked up.
