NEW WAVE KOREAN THRILLER CORPUS
Source: Original screenplay excerpts in the style of 2000s Korean crime cinema
Genre: Thriller | Era: New Wave | Region: Korean

EXT. RICE PADDY - GYUNGGI PROVINCE - DAWN

Fog. The paddy is green and still. A CROW
lands on a fence post and stares.

DETECTIVE PARK DU-MAN, 40s, stands at the edge
of the paddy in rubber boots. He's been standing
here for twenty minutes, looking at something in
the water.

His partner, DETECTIVE SEO TAE-YOON, 30s, approaches
from the road. City shoes. Wrong shoes for this.

                    SEO
          Third one.

                    PARK
          Third one.

They look at the water together.

                    SEO
          Same binding?

                    PARK
          Same binding. Same hands. Same everything.

                    SEO
          Except we still don't have anyone.

                    PARK
          Except that.

INT. POLICE STATION - HWASEONG - DAY

A whiteboard covered in photographs and strings.
Officers moving between desks. The CHIEF, 55,
stands in the doorway.

                    CHIEF
          Seoul is sending someone.

                    PARK
          We don't need Seoul.

                    CHIEF
          Seoul disagrees.

                    PARK
          With respect sir, when Seoul drove out
          here last time they couldn't find the
          town hall with a map.

                    CHIEF
          Nonetheless.
                    (pause)
          His name is Seo. He's bringing a profiler.

Park looks at his board. All those photographs.
He's been living with these women's faces
for eight months.

                    PARK
          A profiler.

                    CHIEF
          That's what they do in America.

                    PARK
          This isn't America.

EXT. COUNTRY ROAD - EVENING

Park drives. Rain starting. He passes the same
rice paddies, the same farm gates, the same
dogs watching from yards.

He's driven this road three hundred times.
He knows every house. He's been inside most of them.

He parks on a hill and looks at the town below.
Somewhere down there, someone knows something.
Or someone is someone.

He lights a cigarette. He'll sit here a while.
He does this sometimes.

INT. LOCAL RESTAURANT - NIGHT

Park and Seo. Two bottles of soju between them,
one mostly empty. A plate of pajeon getting cold.

                    SEO
          The profiler says the perpetrator is
          organized. Local knowledge. Comfortable
          in this environment.

                    PARK
          Brilliant.

                    SEO
          He says he may have been in the military.

                    PARK
          Half the men in this province were
          in the military.

                    SEO
          It narrows it down.

                    PARK
          To about fifty thousand people.

Seo picks up his glass.

                    SEO
          You have a better approach?

                    PARK
          I have been interviewing people for
          eight months. I walk these roads.
          I drink this soju and I listen.

                    SEO
          And?

                    PARK
          And I know who did it.

Seo sets his glass down.

                    SEO
          Who?

                    PARK
          I know who did it. I just can't prove it.
          He's too careful. Too domestic.
          He mows his lawn. He greets his neighbors.
          He waves at children.

                    SEO
          That could describe anyone.

                    PARK
          Yes. That's the problem.

EXT. SUSPECT'S HOUSE - DAY

A modest house. Vegetable garden. A man, BAEK,
45, is fixing a fence. He looks up when Park's
car pulls up. He doesn't run. He waves.

Park gets out. Walks over.

                    BAEK
          Detective Park. Again.

                    PARK
          Baek-ssi. Working hard.

                    BAEK
          Always. Tea?

                    PARK
          Please.

They go inside. This is the seventeenth time
Park has been to this house.

INT. BAEK'S HOUSE - KITCHEN - DAY

Clean. Orderly. Tea service on the table.
Baek's WIFE, 40s, brings cups. She smiles at Park
the way you smile at someone you've seen too often.

                    PARK
          Do you remember the night of September 15th?

                    BAEK
          You've asked me this before, Detective.

                    PARK
          I know. I'm asking again.

                    BAEK
          I was home. My wife was here. We watched
          television. A drama. I fell asleep.

                    WIFE
          He always falls asleep during the drama.

She smiles again. Park smiles back.

He drinks his tea. The kitchen is so clean.

INT. POLICE STATION - NIGHT

Park pins a new photograph to the board.
He draws a line. He looks at all the lines.

Seo comes in.

                    SEO
          He has an alibi for the third night too.

                    PARK
          I know.

                    SEO
          Solid. Three witnesses.

                    PARK
          I know.

                    SEO
          Park-ssi. Maybe it's not him.

Park looks at the board for a long time.

                    PARK
          You ever know something that you
          can't unknow? You look at a person
          and you just — know?

                    SEO
          In my experience, that feeling
          has been wrong about half the time.

                    PARK
          In my experience, the half where
          it's wrong is the half that gets
          innocent men locked up.

He takes his coat and goes.

EXT. RICE PADDY - DAWN - MONTHS LATER

Fog again. The crow is back or a different crow.

Park stands at the edge again. Different water.

Another one.

He doesn't move for a long time.
Behind him, on the road, Seo's headlights arrive.

                    SEO
                    (from a distance)
          Park-ssi!

Park knows what he's going to say.
He knew when his phone rang at 4am.
He knew when he drove out here in the dark.

He's been knowing things for a year and a half.
He's been unable to do anything about them.

He turns to let Seo come towards him.
The fog is very still.

EXT. RICE FIELD - DAWN

DETECTIVE PARK stands in the mud, looking down. Another body. The third this month. He has no leads and the mud is ruining his shoes.

His partner DETECTIVE SEO photographs the scene.

                    SEO
          Same as the others?

                    PARK
          Same field. Different victim.

                    SEO
          Maybe it's the field.

Park looks at him.

                    SEO
          I'm not joking. Maybe something
          about this specific location.

INT. VILLAGE POLICE STATION - NIGHT

Park pins photographs to the board. Three victims. Three different lives. One field.

He stares at the photographs for a long time.

                    PARK
          What do you have in common?

The photographs do not answer.

EXT. FIELD - NIGHT

Park stands alone in the field in the dark. He has come back without telling anyone. Just to stand here. To see what it feels like.

It feels like nothing. Cold. Mud. Silence.

Then a sound. Footsteps. Someone else is in the field.

Park does not move.
