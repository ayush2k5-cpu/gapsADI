CLASSIC INDIAN ROMANCE CORPUS
Source: Original screenplay excerpts in the style of 1950s-60s Mughal/Raj-era Bollywood romance
Genre: Romance | Era: Classic/Golden Age | Region: Indian

INT. MUGHAL COURT - DIWAN-E-KHAS - DAY

Marble columns. Latticed screens. Beyond them,
women's voices and the sound of sitar.

PRINCE SALIM, 25, stands before his father the EMPEROR.
The court is full. Everyone is watching without
appearing to watch.

                    EMPEROR
          You were seen again. Near the zenana walls.

                    SALIM
          I was walking in the gardens, Father.
          As I always do.

                    EMPEROR
          You were not always alone in those gardens.

A silence. The court pretends to be made of stone.

                    SALIM
                    (quietly)
          Anarkali is a court musician. She sings
          for the court. That is not a crime.

                    EMPEROR
          It is when the one listening is my son.
          And heir.

EXT. PALACE GARDEN - EVENING

The gardens at the golden hour. ANARKALI, 22,
walks with her head covered, moving quickly.

Salim falls into step beside her. She doesn't
stop walking.

                    ANARKALI
          You should not be here, Shahzade.

                    SALIM
          Then I am a fool.

                    ANARKALI
          Yes. A foolish prince with nothing to lose
          and everything to give away.

                    SALIM
          You think I have nothing to lose?

She stops. Faces him for the first time. Her eyes
tell the truth even when her words don't.

                    ANARKALI
          You have an empire to lose.
          I have only my life.

                    SALIM
          Empires can be rebuilt. Some things
          cannot be.

INT. COURT MUSICIAN'S QUARTERS - NIGHT

Anarkali sits with her COMPANION, Dilnawaz.

                    DILNAWAZ
          The Empress sent a message. You are
          to be given new quarters. Farther
          from the gardens.

                    ANARKALI
          I understand.

                    DILNAWAZ
          Anarkali. This ends only one way.

                    ANARKALI
          Which way is that?

                    DILNAWAZ
          With you weeping. Or worse.

Anarkali looks at the lamp between them.

                    ANARKALI
          I have been careful with my heart
          my whole life, Dilnawaz. I have
          been so careful. And then—

She doesn't finish. Dilnawaz takes her hand.

INT. DIWAN-E-AAM - COURT - DAY

The Emperor sits in full court. Courtiers arranged
in their ranks. Anarkali is brought forward.

She walks with her head high. She does not look
at Salim, who stands at the far end of the hall.

But he cannot stop looking at her.

                    EMPEROR
          You have been accused of conduct unbecoming—

                    ANARKALI
          I have been accused of loving, Jahanpanah.
          Nothing more. Nothing less.

A murmur through the court.

                    EMPEROR
          Love between a court musician and the Crown
          Prince is treason.

                    ANARKALI
          Then I am a traitor. But I am not ashamed.

Salim takes a step forward.

                    SALIM
          Father—

                    EMPEROR
                    (sharp)
          Be still.

Salim is still. There are some commands even love
cannot override. Not in this court.

EXT. PALACE WALLS - NIGHT

Salim stands on the ramparts alone. Below him:
the city. Lights in a thousand windows.
Somewhere in this palace, Anarkali waits.

He has the power to raise armies. He cannot
raise a hand against his father.

INT. EMPRESS'S CHAMBER - NIGHT

The EMPRESS sits at her mirror. She is still
beautiful. Still dangerous.

                    EMPRESS
          You think I am cruel.

Salim has been brought here. He stands rigid.

                    SALIM
          I think you are afraid.

She looks at him in the mirror. A long pause.

                    EMPRESS
          Yes. I am afraid. I am afraid of what
          a common girl's love will cost my son.
          Cost this empire.

                    SALIM
          You were not always an empress, Mother.
          Once you were only a girl someone loved.

She turns from the mirror. Something passes
over her face that she won't let become tears.

                    EMPRESS
          That girl made a choice. A hard one.
          And I ask the same of you.

INT. UNDERGROUND CHAMBER - NIGHT

A single torch. Anarkali sits against a stone wall.
Her hands are folded in her lap. She's not crying.

A GUARD brings water. She thanks him.

Then she begins to sing. Softly.

The sound travels up through the stone.
In the garden above, a night-watchman stops walking.
He listens for a moment. Then he moves on.

EXT. PALACE - DAWN

Salim rides out of the gate on a horse. Four
soldiers ride with him. He doesn't look back.

He's made a choice. He doesn't know yet
what it will cost.

INT. THRONE ROOM - DAY

The Emperor alone, after court has cleared.

His advisor BIRBAL approaches.

                    BIRBAL
          Your son has ridden to the Northern garrison.
          He is raising troops.

The Emperor does not move. He looks at his own
hands for a long moment.

                    EMPEROR
          He will lose.

                    BIRBAL
          Yes, Jahanpanah. He will lose.

                    EMPEROR
          And when he does—

                    BIRBAL
          He will stand before you in chains.
          Your own son.

The Emperor rises. Goes to the window.
The city stretches away forever in the morning light.

                    EMPEROR
                    (very quietly)
          There must be another way.

He's not asking Birbal. He's asking himself.
He doesn't have an answer yet.

EXT. RIVER BANK - DAWN

MEERA kneels at the water's edge, filling a clay pot. The sun rises behind the temple. She does not look at RAJAN, standing ten feet away, pretending to fix his bicycle.

                    MEERA
          The wheel is on the wrong side.

                    RAJAN
          I know.

He does not move. Neither does she.

INT. HAVELI COURTYARD - NIGHT

Lanterns. A function. Meera dances in the centre. Rajan watches from the archway. Her father watches Rajan.
