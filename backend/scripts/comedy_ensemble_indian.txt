COMEDY ENSEMBLE INDIAN CORPUS
Source: Original screenplay excerpts in the style of 1990s-2000s Hindi ensemble comedy
Genre: Comedy | Era: Commercial Peak | Region: Indian

INT. RUNDOWN APARTMENT - MUMBAI - MORNING

A tiny flat shared by three men who have no business
sharing a flat.

BABURAO, 45, landlord and aspiring criminal mastermind,
is making tea in the kitchen. The kitchen is the
living room. Also the dining room.

SHYAM, 28, thin and nervous, is still on the floor
trying to read a newspaper. RAJU, 26, has his
feet on Shyam's newspaper.

                    BABURAO
          Today. Today is the day.

                    SHYAM
          You said that yesterday.

                    BABURAO
          Yesterday was yesterday. Today is today.

                    RAJU
          What's today?

                    BABURAO
          Today we become rich men.

                    SHYAM
          Last time you said that we ended
          up in Thane with a goat.

                    BABURAO
          That goat was an investment.

                    RAJU
          The goat ate my jacket.

                    BABURAO
          Your jacket was not an investment.

INT. NEARBY CHAWL - CORRIDOR - DAY

They are following a man. The man does not know
he is being followed. He carries a briefcase.

                    BABURAO
                    (whispering)
          That briefcase. That briefcase
          has sixty thousand rupees in it.

                    SHYAM
                    (whispering)
          How do you know?

                    BABURAO
          Pappu the Pan-wallah told me.

                    RAJU
                    (whispering)
          Pappu the Pan-wallah doesn't know
          sixty rupees from sixty thousand.

The man with the briefcase stops. He turns around.
All three of them stop and look at the ceiling.

                    MAN
          Can I help you?

                    BABURAO
          No, no. We live here.

                    MAN
          This is my building.

                    BABURAO
          Which floor?

                    MAN
          All of them. I own this building.

A very long pause.

                    BABURAO
          Congratulations.

They walk away in the opposite direction.
Rapidly.

EXT. BUS STOP - DAY

Three men at a bus stop looking as innocent
as three men with a plan can look.

                    RAJU
          New plan?

                    BABURAO
          I'm thinking.

                    SHYAM
          Last time you thought, we ended
          up in custody in Virar.

                    BABURAO
          That was not my fault. The chickens
          were already on the bus.

                    RAJU
          Why do all your plans involve animals?

                    BABURAO
          Animals are reliable. People are not.

A BUS arrives. They all three attempt to board.
They all three get stuck in the door simultaneously.
The BUS DRIVER waits.

                    BUS DRIVER
                    (very tired)
          One at a time, bhai.

INT. HAIR SALON - DAY

Shyam has been talked into cutting someone's hair.
He has never cut anyone's hair in his life.

The CLIENT sits in the chair. Shyam stands
behind him holding scissors with the expression
of a man who knows this is going wrong.

                    CLIENT
          You're the new one?

                    SHYAM
          Very new. Almost brand new.

                    CLIENT
          The other fellow?

                    SHYAM
          Indisposed.

                    CLIENT
          Sick?

                    SHYAM
          Arrested. But briefly.

The client half-rises.

                    CLIENT
          Arrested?

                    SHYAM
          Minor misunderstanding. Nothing to do
          with haircutting.
                    (pause)
          Mostly.

                    CLIENT
          How much experience do you have?

Shyam looks at the scissors. Then at the mirror.

                    SHYAM
          On hair?

                    CLIENT
          YES. ON HAIR.

                    SHYAM
          Some. Earlier. Today. On someone else.
          They seemed happy.
                    (pause)
          Well. One of them. The other one is
          still deciding. You look like you
          have very forgiving hair.

INT. POLICE STATION - DAY

Baburao sits across from INSPECTOR CHULBUL PANDEY,
who looks like he has seen people like Baburao
every day of his career and finds them
simultaneously exhausting and inevitable.

                    CHULBUL
          Baburao. Again.

                    BABURAO
          Inspector sahib. I was simply—

                    CHULBUL
          If the next word is 'walking' I will
          personally arrest your shadow.

                    BABURAO
          I was assisting a citizen.

                    CHULBUL
          You were in a bank.

                    BABURAO
          I was assisting a citizen of a bank.

                    CHULBUL
          With a bag over your shoulder.

                    BABURAO
          I carry my lunch in a bag.

                    CHULBUL
          Your lunch was a wad of cash rubber-banded
          to a note that said 'property of bank.'

                    BABURAO
          I found it.

                    CHULBUL
          In the vault.

                    BABURAO
          Someone had left it there.

Very long pause.

                    CHULBUL
          Baburao.

                    BABURAO
          Inspector sahib.

                    CHULBUL
          I'm going to let you go.
          And you know why I'm going to let you go?

                    BABURAO
          My innocent demeanour?

                    CHULBUL
          Because charging you involves paperwork
          and I've had a long day. Go.

                    BABURAO
          With the lunch?

                    CHULBUL
          WITHOUT THE LUNCH.

EXT. STREET OUTSIDE POLICE STATION - DAY

Baburao exits. Raju and Shyam are waiting.
Shyam is holding scissors.

                    BABURAO
          Why do you have scissors?

                    SHYAM
          Long story.

                    BABURAO
          Is anyone bleeding?

                    SHYAM
          Not anymore.

                    BABURAO
          Then we move to Plan C.

                    RAJU
          There was no Plan B.

                    BABURAO
          Plan B was a learning experience.
          Plan C is better.

                    RAJU
          What's Plan C?

                    BABURAO
          I'll tell you on the way.

                    SHYAM
          That means he doesn't know yet.

                    BABURAO
          I'm thinking at high speed.
          Walking helps.

They walk. Babulrao slightly in front because
he's thinking. Raju and Shyam behind him.
This is how they always walk.
This is their formation.

They turn a corner into the exact same street
as their building owner.

The building owner sees them.
They see him.

                    BABURAO
          New Plan C: run.

They run.

EXT. CHAWL ROOFTOP - MORNING

PAPPU waters a dead plant with great seriousness. BUNTY emerges from the stairwell, panting.

                    BUNTY
          The money is gone.

                    PAPPU
          Which money?

                    BUNTY
          All of it. All the moneys.

Pappu looks at the dead plant. Looks at Bunty.

                    PAPPU
          I told you to keep it under the
          mattress.

                    BUNTY
          The mattress is also gone.

INT. POLICE STATION - DAY

Inspector TAWDE, 50s, does not look up from his newspaper.

                    TAWDE
          Sit.

They sit. A long silence.

                    TAWDE
          You want to report a theft.

                    PAPPU
          Two thefts. The money and
          the mattress.

                    TAWDE
          Same thief?

                    BUNTY
          We assume so.

EXT. BAZAAR - DAY

They spot the mattress immediately. It is being sold at a stall. A man sits on it, testing the springs.

                    PAPPU
          That is our mattress.

                    BUNTY
          And that is our money in his pocket.

They look at each other.

                    PAPPU
          We need a plan.

                    BUNTY
          I have no plans. I never have plans.
          Plans are your department.

                    PAPPU
          My department is closed.

INT. SHETH LANDLORD HOUSE - DAY

SHETH, 60s, mustache, throne-like chair, overdue notices everywhere.

                    SHETH
          Two months rent. Two months.

                    BABURAO
          We have it.

                    SHETH
          Where?

                    BABURAO
          Coming by Tuesday.

EXT. SHETH HOUSE - CONTINUOUS

They flee down the stairs.

                    SHYAM
          You said Tuesday?

                    BABURAO
          Next Tuesday. The concept of Tuesday
          is flexible.
