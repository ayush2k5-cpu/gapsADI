ARTHOUSE MINIMAL GLOBAL CORPUS
Source: Original screenplay excerpts in the style of contemporary global arthouse cinema
Genre: Drama/Arthouse | Era: Contemporary | Region: Global

INT. APARTMENT - TOKYO - MORNING

A man sits at a table. He has been sitting here
for some time. There is a cup of tea to his left.
It has gone cold. He has not moved to drink it.

HAMAGUCHI YUSUKE, 50s, looks at a script.
The script has handwritten notes in four colors.

Through the window: the city going about itself.

He picks up a pen. Does not write.
Puts it down.

INT. REHEARSAL SPACE - DAY

Four actors around a table. Scripts.
YUSUKE at the head, but not presiding.

                    ACTOR 1
          "I have been thinking about what you said."

                    YUSUKE
          Stop. What were you thinking about
          before that line?

                    ACTOR 1
          My character was thinking about—

                    YUSUKE
          No. You. What were you thinking about?

A pause.

                    ACTOR 1
          I was trying to remember the line
          after this one.

                    YUSUKE
          Yes. That's the problem.
          Read it again. But this time,
          don't try to remember the next line.

                    ACTOR 1
          "I have been thinking about what you said."

This time it sounds like a sentence spoken by
a human being to another human being.

                    YUSUKE
          There.

EXT. TOKYO STREET - DAY

Yusuke walks. He walks slowly. The city
moves quickly around him. He is a rock
in a river. The river doesn't notice.

He stops at a crossing. The light is red.
He looks at the people next to him. A woman
in a grey coat. A man with a bicycle. A child
pulling a parent's hand.

He thinks about something. We cannot see
what. His face tells us he's thought about
this before.

The light changes. Everyone crosses.
He crosses last.

INT. RESTAURANT - EVENING

A private room. Yusuke eats with his PRODUCER,
who is explaining at some length why the film
cannot be made as written.

                    PRODUCER
          The ending. Nobody will accept the ending.

                    YUSUKE
          The characters accept it.

                    PRODUCER
          The audience needs—

                    YUSUKE
          The audience needs to drive home
          and think about what they've seen.
          For a week. Maybe more.

                    PRODUCER
          The audience needs to drive home, Yusuke.
          Not sit in the parking lot.

                    YUSUKE
          The parking lot is also acceptable.

The producer picks up his sake.

                    PRODUCER
          The actors understood the ending?

                    YUSUKE
          They did after three months.

                    PRODUCER
          We don't have three months for the audience.

                    YUSUKE
          We have the time between when the film ends
          and when the lights come back on.

He eats his fish. This is apparently his final word.

INT. CAR - MOVING - NIGHT

Yusuke is being driven. He looks out the window.
The city lights pass. He's recording something
on his phone — himself, speaking quietly.

                    YUSUKE
                    (into phone)
          The third act problem. If Misaki
          says what she knows, the film ends one way.
          If she stays silent, another way.
          Both ways are honest.
          The question is: what does she know
          about herself? 
          Does she know that silence
          is also a choice?
          Does she know that choosing silence
          is not the same as being silenced?

He stops recording. Looks out the window.

A long road. An overpass. The city going on
in all directions.

He presses record again.

                    YUSUKE (CONT'D)
          She stays silent. 
          That's the film.

INT. RECORDING STUDIO - DAY

The COMPOSER, 40s, plays the film's theme on piano.
It's simple. Barely there.

Yusuke listens with his eyes closed.

The theme ends. Silence.

                    YUSUKE
          Again. But slower. As if you're not sure
          it should be heard.

The theme again. More tentative. A sound the film
makes when it thinks no one is listening.

                    YUSUKE (CONT'D)
          That's it.

EXT. EMPTY THEATER - NIGHT

After everyone has gone. Yusuke sits alone
in the center of the theater.

The screen is white. Nothing on it.

He sits with the white screen for a while.
He's thinking about what goes on it.
He's thinking about what should not.

He thinks about Misaki in the car in the last scene.
The window. The road. The thing she knows
that she will not say.

He thinks about how we only understand silence
when we're surrounded by it.

He stays until the lights go out automatically.
Then he sits in the dark.

Then he goes home.

EXT. BRIDGE - DAWN

A river. A bridge. Early light.

Yusuke stands in the middle of the bridge.
Not looking at the water. Looking at the
other shore.

He's working on the last scene in his head.
He does this.

The light changes. Something is decided.
Something about stillness, about what the
camera should be doing, about whether the
silence that ends the film is empty or full.

He decides it's full.

He decides the audience will feel that.
He decides to trust that they will.

He walks off the bridge. He goes to work.

EXT. EMPTY HIGHWAY - DAWN

Flat land. A car moves in the distance. The camera holds the landscape long after the car is gone.

INT. REST STOP BATHROOM - DAY

A man washes his hands very carefully. Looks at himself in the cracked mirror. Does not recognise what he sees.

EXT. FIELD - DUSK

He walks into the field. Sits. The grass is very still. Nothing happens for a long time. This is the point.
