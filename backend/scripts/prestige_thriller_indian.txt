PRESTIGE THRILLER CONTEMPORARY INDIAN CORPUS
Source: Original screenplay excerpts in the style of 2010s Indian prestige thriller
Genre: Thriller | Era: Contemporary | Region: Indian

INT. POLICE HEADQUARTERS - DELHI - NIGHT

A large room being used as a makeshift war room.
COMMISSIONER VARTIKA CHATURVEDI, 45, stands at
a glass board covered in photographs and maps.

The photographs are of women. Eight of them.
All missing.

Her team: SI BHUPENDRA SINGH, 35, quiet and precise.
DR. NEETI SINGH, psychologist, 40, watching the board.

Nobody is talking. They've been not talking
for four minutes.

                    BHUPENDRA
          The trail goes cold at the bus stand.
          Same as the last three.

                    VARTIKA
          They don't just disappear at a bus stand.

                    BHUPENDRA
          The bus stand has no working CCTV.

                    VARTIKA
          Of course it doesn't.

                    NEETI
          The pattern is consistent. The women
          are all between twenty and thirty-five.
          All employed. All have smartphones.
          All were seen somewhere public just before—

                    VARTIKA
          That's not a narrowing. That describes
          half the working female population
          of Delhi.

                    NEETI
          I know.

Vartika turns from the board. Looks at the window.
The city outside: lit and restless.

                    VARTIKA
          Who knows where these women are?

                    BHUPENDRA
          Someone does.

                    VARTIKA
          Yes. Someone who uses their knowledge
          of this city the way you and I use air.
          Invisibly. Constantly. Everywhere.

EXT. CRIME SCENE - SOUTH DELHI - EARLY MORNING

A colony street. Already cordoned off.
Vartika ducks under the tape.

The FORENSIC OFFICER looks up.

                    FORENSIC OFFICER
          Ma'am. He left more this time.

                    VARTIKA
          More what?

                    FORENSIC OFFICER
          Evidence. Deliberately. Like he
          wants us to see something.

                    VARTIKA
          Or he wants us to see what he
          wants us to see.

She crouches. Looks at the pavement.
She doesn't touch. She reads.

                    VARTIKA (CONT'D)
          He's been here before. Not last night.
          Before. He knows this street.

                    FORENSIC OFFICER
          How can you tell?

                    VARTIKA
          Because he didn't check his sightlines.
          He already knew them.

INT. SUSPECT'S APARTMENT - DAY

RAMAN BHATT, 38, opens his door to Vartika
and Bhupendra. He's a transport company bookkeeper.
Mild. Glasses. The sort of person who becomes
invisible in a crowd.

                    RAMAN
          Yes?

                    VARTIKA
          Mr. Bhatt. We're following up on
          the incident near the Colony bus stand.

                    RAMAN
          Of course. I already spoke to your
          officers last week.

                    VARTIKA
          I know. I just have a few more questions.

He lets them in. His apartment is very clean.
Every book on his shelf is alphabetical.
She notices.

                    VARTIKA
                    (conversational)
          Do you drive, Mr. Bhatt?

                    RAMAN
          I have a two-wheeler. Nothing major.

                    VARTIKA
          And before this job?

                    RAMAN
          I was in logistics. Vans. Distribution.

                    VARTIKA
          You know the city well.

                    RAMAN
          I did that for eight years.
          You tend to learn the shortcuts.

She's looking at his bookshelves while he answers.
She's not looking at his books.
She's reading the distances between things.

                    VARTIKA
          Thank you for your time, Mr. Bhatt.
          Oh — one more thing.

She turns at the door.

                    VARTIKA (CONT'D)
          Your building has a parking level.
          For residents.

                    RAMAN
          It does.

                    VARTIKA
          Do you use it?

                    RAMAN
          For my two-wheeler? No. It's mostly
          for four-wheelers.

                    VARTIKA
          Who else has access?

                    RAMAN
                    (pause)
          Just residents.

                    VARTIKA
          Of course. Thank you.

She goes. In the corridor:

                    BHUPENDRA
                    (very quietly)
          What did you see?

                    VARTIKA
          The gap between the right books and the
          left books. Exactly the width of a folder.

                    BHUPENDRA
          He moved something before letting us in.

                    VARTIKA
          He reorganized his entire shelf.
          Alphabetically. In the forty seconds
          between our knock and the door opening.

EXT. STREETS - MOVING CAR - NIGHT

Vartika in the passenger seat. Bhupendra driving.
They're both quiet.

                    BHUPENDRA
          It might not be him.

                    VARTIKA
          No. But someone rearranges their
          entire bookshelf in forty seconds.

                    BHUPENDRA
          He might just be neat.

                    VARTIKA
          He's too neat. For forty seconds.
          You have to know where everything goes—

She stops. Looks at the street.

                    VARTIKA (CONT'D)
          Pull over.

He pulls over.

                    VARTIKA (CONT'D)
          The bus stand. What time does
          the last Route 14 run?

                    BHUPENDRA
          Eleven-fifteen. Why?

                    VARTIKA
          Because none of our women took Route 14.
          But the bus stand is on the Route 14 line.
          Someone waiting there would look normal.
          Unremarkable.

She gets out of the car. Stands at the kerb.
She's standing where someone stood.
Thinking what someone thought.

                    VARTIKA (CONT'D)
          He waited. He watched which way
          they walked from the stand.
          He knew the single stretch of no cameras.
          He's done this route a hundred times.

She looks up the street. Dark and ordinary.

                    VARTIKA (CONT'D)
          Call Bhatt's logistics company.
          I want his delivery routes.
          For the last eight years.

INT. POLICE HEADQUARTERS - DELHI - 3AM

The board has new strings on it. New connections.
Vartika stands before it alone.

She has eight photographs of missing women.
She has one photograph of Raman Bhatt.

The string between them is red.
                    
She knows. She can't yet prove.
She's been here before. Every case, there is
this moment when you know and can't prove.

She does not go home. She pulls a chair
to the board and sits down.

She'll work until knowing becomes proof.
Or until another photograph goes on the wall.

Neither option is acceptable.
So she keeps working.

INT. HOSPITAL ROOM - DAY

ARJUN SINHA, 40s, bandaged hands, stares at the ceiling. A POLICE OFFICER sits beside the bed. Notebook open.

                    OFFICER
          From the beginning. What were
          you doing on that road?

                    ARJUN
          I was driving home.

                    OFFICER
          At 2am.

                    ARJUN
          I often drive late.

                    OFFICER
          And the other car?

Arjun looks at the ceiling. A long beat.

                    ARJUN
          What other car?

INT. ARJUN'S APARTMENT - NIGHT (ONE WEEK EARLIER)

The same room. But now we see what Arjun cannot tell the officer.

He is not alone. NAINA, 30s, stands at the window.

                    NAINA
          I need you to do something for me.

                    ARJUN
          Anything.

She turns. Her expression tells us this was the wrong answer.

EXT. HIGHWAY - NIGHT

Two cars. Arjun's. And another.

They are not racing. One is following. The other knows it.

INT. ARJUN'S CAR - CONTINUOUS

Arjun checks his mirror. The headlights are still there.

His phone rings. Naina.

                    NAINA (V.O.)
          Where are you?

                    ARJUN
          Being followed. Your people?

                    NAINA (V.O.)
          I don't have people.

The car behind accelerates.

EXT. HIGHWAY - CONTINUOUS

Impact. Controlled. The other car forces Arjun off the road.

He stops. Gets out. The other car is gone.

In his headlights: a briefcase on the roadside. His name written on the tag.

He did not leave it there.

INT. HOSPITAL ROOM - DAY (PRESENT)

The officer closes his notebook.

                    OFFICER
          It's interesting. We found the
          briefcase. Your prints on it.
          And inside — documents with your
          signature. Dated six months ago.

                    ARJUN
          I've never seen that briefcase.

                    OFFICER
          Mr. Sinha. We also spoke to Naina.

Arjun is very still.

                    OFFICER
          She says she's never met you.
