NEW WAVE EUROPEAN DRAMA CORPUS
Source: Original screenplay excerpts in the style of 1960s-70s European new wave realism
Genre: Drama | Era: New Wave | Region: European

EXT. PARIS STREET - MORNING

A boy runs. ANTOINE MOREL, 12, thin and quick,
darts around a corner into a narrow alley.
He stops. Breathes. Then keeps running.

The streets are crowded. Nobody notices one
more running boy.

INT. APARTMENT - KITCHEN - MORNING

ANTOINE'S MOTHER, 35, drinks coffee at the table.
She is dressed for work. She has not combed her hair.
The STEPFATHER reads a newspaper. Nobody speaks.

Antoine comes in. She looks at him.

                    MOTHER
          You're late.

                    ANTOINE
          I know.

                    MOTHER
          Your teacher called yesterday.

He opens the refrigerator. Nothing much in it.

                    ANTOINE
          What did he say?

                    MOTHER
          What do you think he said?

He closes the refrigerator. Takes a piece of bread.

                    ANTOINE
          I'm going to school.

He goes. The stepfather turns a page.
The mother finishes her coffee.

EXT. SCHOOL COURTYARD - DAY

A grey building. Boys lined up. ANTOINE stands
with his friend RENE, both of them at the back.

                    RENE
          Did you do the composition?

                    ANTOINE
          No.

                    RENE
          Morin's going to kill you.

                    ANTOINE
          He's going to try.

INT. CLASSROOM - DAY

PROFESSOR MORIN, 50s, writes on the blackboard.
The classroom is cold. Boys write in notebooks.

Antoine stares out the window. Through the glass:
rooftops, pigeons, a woman hanging washing.

                    MORIN
          Morel. Read your composition.

Antoine looks at his empty notebook.

                    ANTOINE
          I didn't write one, sir.

Silence.

                    MORIN
          Why not?

                    ANTOINE
          My grandmother died. I was at the hospital
          all weekend.

He says it flatly. Morin looks at him for a long moment.

                    MORIN
          Detention.

Because Morin knows. Because everyone in this room knows.
But what can you do with a lie that stands so still?

EXT. CITY STREETS - AFTERNOON

Antoine and Rene, free from school somehow, wander.
They eat stolen pastry. They look in shop windows.
They have no money and the afternoon ahead of them.

                    RENE
          What are you going to do?

                    ANTOINE
          About what?

                    RENE
          Everything.

Antoine watches a man walk out of a building with
a briefcase. Watches a woman with a poodle.
Watches the city move around him like water.

                    ANTOINE
          I don't know.

                    RENE
          Your parents?

                    ANTOINE
          They're not my parents.
                    (pause)
          I mean. My mother is my mother.
          But she didn't want me. I heard her say it.
          To him. She said she hadn't wanted me.

Rene says nothing. What is there to say?

INT. CINEMA - EVENING

Dark. A film playing. Antoine watches the screen.
His face in the light from the projector: open,
absorbed, for once completely present.

On screen, something funny happens.

Antoine laughs. He laughs entirely, without thinking.
For a moment he is just a boy in a cinema watching
something funny.

Then the moment passes. He is himself again.

EXT. APARTMENT BUILDING - NIGHT

Antoine sits on the steps. It is late. He doesn't
want to go up. He's not sure what's waiting.

A NEIGHBOR comes out with a dog.

                    NEIGHBOR
          Still here?

                    ANTOINE
          Just sitting.

She goes. Antoine sits a while longer.
Then he goes up.

INT. DETENTION ROOM - DAY

Antoine writes lines. The room is empty except
for the SUPERVISOR, who reads a magazine.

Antoine writes: "I must not lie to my teachers."
He writes it forty times. By the twentieth time
he stops reading the words. His hand keeps moving.

                    ANTOINE (V.O.)
          The worst thing about being caught in a lie
          is that you stop knowing what the truth is.
          You say something and you can't remember
          if you made it up or if it happened.

EXT. REFORMATORY - DAY

Grey walls. A yard. Antoine stands at the fence
looking out at the sea. The water goes all the way
to the edge of the world.

He's never seen the sea before.

He walks toward it. Nobody stops him.
He walks into the water up to his ankles.
The cold hits him.

He turns back toward land. The reformatory
is behind him. The sea in front.

He looks at the camera. Or at the horizon.
It's hard to say which.

He's still looking.

EXT. PARIS STREET - MORNING

ANTOINE walks to school. Takes a side street. A longer way. He passes the bakery, the cinema, the woman who feeds pigeons every morning. He is in no hurry to arrive.

INT. SCHOOL CLASSROOM - DAY

The teacher asks a question. Antoine does not know the answer. He did not do the reading. He stares at the window.

Outside: the street. A man walking a dog. The man looks up at the window. Antoine looks back. The man looks away. Antoine looks away.

                    TEACHER
          Doinel. The question.

                    ANTOINE
          I don't know, sir.

                    TEACHER
          Then perhaps you should have
          done the reading.

Antoine sits. The window is still there. The street is still there.

EXT. SEINE RIVERBANK - DUSK

Antoine sits on the stone wall. Throws pebbles. Watches them disappear.

A barge passes. A man on the barge reads a newspaper. He does not look up as he passes.

Antoine watches until the barge is gone.
