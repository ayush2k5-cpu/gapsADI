CLASSIC INDIAN DRAMA CORPUS
Source: Original screenplay excerpts in the style of 1950s-60s Indian social realism
Genre: Drama | Era: Classic/Golden Age | Region: Indian

EXT. VILLAGE - WHEAT FIELDS - DAWN

A woman's hands in the soil. RADHA, 40s, weathered
and strong, pulls weeds from the dry earth.
Around her: a village waking up. Smoke from mud
hearths. Children heading to the well.

                    RADHA (V.O.)
          The land does not ask why you are tired.
          It only asks if you will come back to it
          tomorrow.

EXT. ZAMINDAR'S COURTYARD - DAY

SUKHILALA, the moneylender, 55, sits on a charpoy
under a neem tree. His accounts book is open. He
doesn't look up as Radha approaches.

                    SUKHILALA
          This month's payment is short again.

                    RADHA
          The rains failed. You know this.

                    SUKHILALA
          The rains failed for everyone. Only you
          come to me with excuses.

                    RADHA
          My husband is ill. My sons are still young.
          Give me until harvest.

He looks up finally. Studies her face.

                    SUKHILALA
          I've been giving you until harvest for
          three harvests now, Radha. 
          At some point, patience becomes foolishness.

INT. RADHA'S HOME - NIGHT

A single lamp. Her husband SHAMU lies on a cot,
thin and feverish. Her two sons, BIRJU (10) and
RAMU (12), sleep nearby.

Radha sits grinding grain. She will grind all night.

                    SHAMU
                    (weakly)
          You should sleep.

                    RADHA
          When there is grain to grind.

                    SHAMU
          The boys need their mother rested.

                    RADHA
          The boys need their mother alive.
          That means grain. That means working.

She doesn't stop grinding. He watches her.
This is the kind of love that doesn't have
words for itself.

EXT. CANAL - DAY

Radha and the village women carry water pots.
The canal is low. Another bad sign.

                    CHAMPA
                    (to Radha)
          Sukhilala has been talking to the other
          women. He says you owe the most of anyone.

                    RADHA
          I owe what I owe. Nobody else's business.

                    CHAMPA
          He said — 
                    (she hesitates)
          He said he'd cancel the debt.
          For a consideration.

Radha sets her pot down. Very carefully.

                    RADHA
          What consideration.

Champa says nothing. Radha picks her pot back up.
Walks away. That is the answer.

EXT. VILLAGE ROAD - AFTERNOON

Birju watches the zamindar's son ride past on a
white horse. The boy on the horse doesn't see Birju.
Why would he?

Birju watches until the horse disappears around
the bend. Something in his eyes that shouldn't be
there yet. He's ten years old.

INT. SCHOOL - DAY

The TEACHER writes on a blackboard. A handful of
children sit on mats. Birju is there, but not there.
He's looking out the window at the men working the
zamindar's main fields. The good fields.
The fields his grandfather used to own.

                    TEACHER
          Birju. What is eight times nine?

                    BIRJU
                    (without looking back)
          Seventy-two, sir.

The teacher nods. Birju goes back to the window.

EXT. WELL - DUSK

Radha carries two full pots. A long walk home still.
She passes the zamindar's main house. Music from
inside — someone celebrating something.

She doesn't stop walking.

INT. RADHA'S HOME - NIGHT

Birju sits up while everyone else sleeps.

                    BIRJU
                    (whisper)
          Amma. Why don't we own the fields anymore?

Radha is silent for a long moment.

                    RADHA
          Your grandfather borrowed money when
          your father was born. For a celebration.
          For happiness.
                    (pause)
          Happiness can be expensive.

                    BIRJU
          We were celebrating and he took the fields?

                    RADHA
          He waited. He was patient. That's how
          the moneylender works, Birju. He waits
          until you cannot wait anymore.

                    BIRJU
          That's not right.

                    RADHA
          No. But it is how things are.

EXT. VILLAGE FESTIVAL - NIGHT

Diwali lamps everywhere. Children run with sparklers.
Radha watches from the edge of the courtyard.
She has one good sari on. Tonight she lets herself
stand still for a minute. Just a minute.

Sukhilala approaches. She moves away before he reaches her.

EXT. TRAIN STATION - VILLAGE - DAY

Shamu is being taken away. He's too sick now.
A city hospital. The sons watch the train.
Ramu cries. Birju does not.

                    RAMU
          Will he come back?

Radha puts her hand on his head.

                    RADHA
          Yes.

She doesn't know if it's true. She says it anyway.
Because some lies are an act of love.

EXT. WHEAT FIELDS - HARVEST TIME - DAWN

A good harvest, finally. The village is working.
Radha among them, cutting, bundling, carrying.
Ramu works beside her. He's grown. Birju is
faster, stronger, angrier.

                    BIRJU
          This harvest — half of it goes to Sukhilala.

                    RADHA
          That's the arrangement.

                    BIRJU
          It'll always be the arrangement. Every harvest.
          Every year. Forever.

                    RADHA
          Birju—

                    BIRJU
          He has our land, Amma. We work it
          and pay him for the privilege.
          When does it end?

Radha looks at the fields. The gold of the grain.

                    RADHA
          When we have paid what we owe.

                    BIRJU
          We will always owe. That's how he
          built it. So we would always owe.

She has no answer. Because he is right.

EXT. SUKHILALA'S HOUSE - NIGHT

Birju, now 18 and burning with it, stands at the gate.
His mother appears behind him, grabbing his arm.

                    RADHA
          Come home.

                    BIRJU
          He took our land. He takes our grain.
          He disrespected you in front of the village.

                    RADHA
          Come home, Birju.

                    BIRJU
          There has to be a reckoning, Amma.
          A man cannot just take and take and—

                    RADHA
          There will be a reckoning.
                    (quietly)
          Just not tonight. Not like this.

She holds his arm. He doesn't move toward the gate.
But he doesn't come home either. Not yet.

EXT. VILLAGE - FLOOD - DAY

The river has broken. Water everywhere. 
People running. Animals panicking.
Radha and Birju are separated in the chaos.

                    RADHA
                    (calling out)
          Birju! BIRJU!

The water rises around her knees. Around her waist.
She keeps calling.

INT. VILLAGE TEMPLE - NIGHT

The survivors gather. Candles. Children wrapped
in dry cloth. The priest praying.

Radha sits against a pillar. She has lost count
of what she has survived. She's not sure
how many more times she can do it.

But she knows she will.

EXT. VILLAGE - AFTER THE FLOOD - MORNING

The village is damaged. The crops are damaged.
But not destroyed. Not completely.

Radha walks through the mud. Stops at the edge
of the field. Puts her hand in the soil.

Still good soil. Still hers in some way.
In the way that matters.

She begins to work.
